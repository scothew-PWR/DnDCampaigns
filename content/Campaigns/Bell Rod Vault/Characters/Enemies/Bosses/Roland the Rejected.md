## Overview

Roland the Rejected is one of the Bell Rod Vault’s recurring champion-level enemies and one of the party’s most physically imposing combat threats.

Where some enemies rely on stealth or magic, Roland represents something much simpler and more brutal:

Relentless martial violence.

Roland excels at sustained pressure, punishing positioning mistakes, and overwhelming targets through sheer physical dominance.

He feels like the type of enemy who keeps advancing no matter how much punishment he takes.

That persistence makes him terrifying.

## Appearance

Roland carries the unmistakable presence of a hardened warrior.

Known characteristics:

- powerful physical build
    
- battle-ready equipment
    
- intimidating stature
    
- veteran fighter energy
    
- commanding presence
    

He gives the impression of someone highly experienced in combat.

Not flashy.

Just lethal.

## Personality

Core traits:

- confident
    
- aggressive
    
- relentless
    
- disciplined
    
- intimidating
    

Roland does not come across as theatrical like Claude or smug like Felix.

He feels grounded.

Dangerously grounded.

There is a directness to him.

He knows violence.  
He trusts violence.  
He applies violence efficiently.

The title **“the Rejected”** hints at a deeper history of loss, betrayal, or exile.

That history remains largely unknown.

## Known History

Roland is one of the Four Champions and one of the party’s earliest recurring major threats.

He fought alongside:

- [[Felix the Flayer]]
    
- [[Claude the Clown]]
    
- [[Vanguard 7]]
    

Together they formed one of the deadliest opposing forces the party has encountered.

The party encountered Roland multiple times.

Each encounter reinforced his threat as a frontline combat monster.

## Early Encounter

### Guild Hall Meeting

One of the party’s earliest strange encounters involving Roland happened at the guild hall.

At the time, the party did not fully realize who they were dealing with.

This led to one of the campaign’s funniest moments.

[[Ephram]] climbed onto [[Mordikahn]]’s shoulders to intimidate a suspicious figure.

That figure later turned out to be Roland.

There was also mead involved.

In hindsight, attempting intimidation through stacked party members and alcohol against a future champion is extremely on-brand for this group.

## Combat Role

Roland appears optimized for:

- frontline combat
    
- sustained damage
    
- pressure
    
- punishment
    
- durability
    

He thrives in extended engagements.

### Key Strengths

Roland excels at:

- direct melee pressure
    
- punishing overextension
    
- exploiting vulnerable targets
    
- supporting allied pressure
    

He becomes especially dangerous when paired with other champions.

For example:

- Felix isolates
    
- Claude controls
    
- Roland punishes
    

That synergy made the champion group exceptionally dangerous.

## Threat Assessment

**Threat Level: Very High**

Roland is dangerous because he applies constant pressure.

He does not need complicated mechanics to be deadly.

He simply keeps coming.

Against weakened or isolated targets, Roland becomes brutal.

## Relationships

### Champions

Roland functioned as the primary martial enforcer among the Four Champions.

His role in the team:

- frontline bruiser
    
- physical pressure
    
- threat anchor
    

He created space for:

- Claude’s casting
    
- Felix’s flanking
    
- Vanguard’s pressure
    

---

### Party

Roland developed into a major recurring threat to the group.

Repeated encounters elevated him from “dangerous enemy” to “personal adversary.”

## Memorable Moments

### Early Suspicion

Roland’s early guild hall encounter remains iconic because the party had no idea how dangerous he would later become.

The absurdity of that moment aged beautifully.

---

### Pressure on Zava

During the Four Champions battle, Roland helped focus devastating pressure onto [[Zava]] after Zava aggressively engaged [[Claude the Clown]].

This nearly overwhelmed the barbarian.

It showcased Roland’s strength as a punisher.

---

### Final Defeat

After the fall of Felix and Claude, Roland remained one of the final major threats.

The party eventually brought him down through sustained effort.

But Roland’s story did not end there.

---

### Undead Resurrection

After Roland’s defeat, [[Devon]] used the **Bangles of the Undead**.

Roland was resurrected under party control.

This instantly transformed a former boss into a temporary ally.

It remains one of the most insane tactical reversals in the campaign.

Roland went from:

> terrifying champion

to

> temporary undead backup

That is objectively hilarious.

He never got his helm back.
