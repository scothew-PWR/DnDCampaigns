## Overview

Vanguard #7 is one of the Four Champions encountered within the Bell Rod Vault and serves as the group’s precision combat platform.

Unlike the other champions, Vanguard #7 does not fight through emotion, cruelty, or ego.

It fights through optimization.

Vanguard behaves like a purpose-built war machine engineered to identify threats, establish firing lanes, and eliminate targets with ruthless efficiency.

It does not waste movement.  
It does not waste attacks.  
It does not hesitate.

It acquires targets and executes.

That cold efficiency makes it terrifying.

## Appearance

Vanguard #7 appears as a highly advanced Warforged combat construct.

Known features:

- armored mechanical frame
    
- reinforced combat chassis
    
- precision-engineered limbs
    
- weapon integration points
    
- highly stable firing posture
    
- advanced sensory systems
    

Unlike bulky siege constructs, Vanguard appears built for mobility and accuracy.

Everything about its frame suggests tactical combat specialization.

Its design feels military.

Purpose-built.

Deadly.

## Personality

Assigning personality to Vanguard #7 is difficult.

Observed traits:

- analytical
    
- efficient
    
- emotionless
    
- disciplined
    
- methodical
    

Vanguard does not appear malicious.

That almost makes it worse.

It doesn’t hate.

It doesn’t rage.

It simply calculates probabilities and acts on optimal outcomes.

This gives it an unnerving presence.

It feels less like fighting a creature and more like fighting combat software.

## Known History

Vanguard #7 is one of the Four Champions who repeatedly opposed the party.

It fought alongside:

- [[Felix the Flayer]]
    
- [[Claude the Clown]]
    
- [[Roland the Rejected]]
    

Within this team, Vanguard served as long-range pressure and tactical elimination support.

Its presence dramatically improved champion battlefield control.

## Combat Role

Vanguard #7 appears optimized for:

- ranged elimination
    
- tactical repositioning
    
- precision targeting
    
- sustained pressure
    
- target prioritization
    

Mechanically, Vanguard resembled a:

**Warforged Ranger 8 / Fighter 6**  
(Total Level 14)

That combination is brutal.

## Combat Style

### Ranger Precision

Vanguard demonstrated traits associated with elite ranger combat:

- long-range threat projection
    
- target tracking
    
- battlefield awareness
    
- priority target marking
    
- kill-zone control
    

It excelled at identifying vulnerable targets.

---

### Fighter Burst

Its fighter training made it significantly more dangerous.

Possible advantages included:

- multiple attacks
    
- burst damage sequences
    
- Action Surge-level tempo swings
    
- superior weapon discipline
    

This meant Vanguard could rapidly escalate from pressure to lethal threat.

One round of focus fire from Vanguard could become catastrophic.

## Threat Assessment

**Threat Level: Very High**

Vanguard was dangerous because it punished exposure.

If you:

- broke formation
    
- lost cover
    
- exposed a caster
    
- overextended
    

Vanguard could capitalize instantly.

Unlike brute enemies, Vanguard killed through discipline.

It made mistakes expensive.

## Relationships

---

### [[Ephram]]

One of the most important moments in the Four Champions fight came when [[Ephram]] successfully charmed Vanguard.

This was massive.

Instead of functioning as enemy ranged support, Vanguard temporarily shifted away from hostile action.

That dramatically reduced incoming pressure.

This likely prevented serious casualties.

---

### [[Dilbert]]

After Vanguard’s defeat, its story took a bizarre turn.

[[Dilbert]] salvaged parts from Vanguard and converted them into a mechanical exosuit.

So part of Vanguard literally lives on as Dilbert’s armor.

A strange but fitting fate.
