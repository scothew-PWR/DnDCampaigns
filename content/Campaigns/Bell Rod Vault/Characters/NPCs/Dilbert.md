## Overview

Dilbert is one of the strangest, most lovable, and most terrifying entities encountered in the Bell Rod Vault.

At first glance, Dilbert appears harmless—an adorable orange-and-black caterpillar-like creature with huge glossy eyes and an almost absurdly innocent appearance.

This is dangerously misleading.

Beneath the cute exterior lies a brilliant engineer, arcane inventor, and magical powerhouse capable of solving impossible problems and unleashing devastating force when necessary.

Dilbert embodies one of the campaign’s greatest contradictions:

> adorable fuzzball  
> versus  
> walking war crime

Both descriptions are accurate.

## Appearance

### Base Form

Dilbert’s natural form resembles a small caterpillar-like creature.

Known features:

- orange and black coloration
    
- fuzzy body
    
- extremely small stature
    
- large expressive glossy eyes
    
- highly animated facial expressions
    

He appears almost toy-like in size and demeanor.

Most people’s first instinct is to underestimate him.

That is usually a mistake.

---

### Armor Form

Dilbert later created a mechanical exosuit using salvaged parts from [[Vanguard 7]].

This transformed him from adorable gremlin into mechanical nightmare.

Known armor features:

- reinforced plating
    
- compact exoskeleton
    
- four arms
    
- four legs
    
- enhanced mobility
    
- heavy defensive upgrades
    

The resulting suit feels like a blend of:

- arcane mech
    
- combat exosuit
    
- murder insect
    
- magical engineering masterpiece
    

In armor, Dilbert becomes dramatically more intimidating.

## Personality

Dilbert balances several extremes that should not logically coexist, yet somehow do.

Core traits:

- brilliant
    
- curious
    
- quirky
    
- lovable
    
- unpredictable
    
- frighteningly capable
    

He often behaves with childlike enthusiasm and innocent excitement.

At the same time, he casually demonstrates intelligence and magical capability far beyond what his appearance suggests.

Dilbert often does not seem to fully appreciate how terrifying his abilities are to everyone around him.

He can go from:

> “This salad is nice.”

to

> “I solved your impossible magical engineering problem.”

with no transition.

## Known History

The party encountered Dilbert in the dungeon city, one of the Bell Rod Vault’s most important turning points.

This city was also where the party encountered:

- [[Thaddeus]]
    
- [[Gork]]
    

Major lore revelations occurred here.

Dilbert quickly became one of the party’s most valuable allies.

Though physically tiny, his impact has been enormous.

## Role in the Party

Dilbert serves multiple critical functions.

### Engineer

Dilbert excels at:

- design
    
- construction
    
- repair
    
- modification
    
- systems analysis
    

Complex mechanical problems often seem intuitive to him.

---

### Arcane Specialist

Dilbert demonstrates extraordinary magical competence.

Observed strengths include:

- magical analysis
    
- spellcasting
    
- artifact interaction
    
- arcane engineering
    
- problem solving
    

---

### Problem Solver

This may be his greatest contribution.

When the party encounters something absurdly difficult, Dilbert is often the one most likely to understand it.

## Magical Capabilities

Dilbert’s upper power ceiling remains unknown.

Observed or inferred capabilities:

- advanced spellcasting
    
- artifact manipulation
    
- magical engineering
    
- construct integration
    
- high-level intelligence
    

The fact that the party still doesn’t know his true limit is… unsettling.

## Mechanical Armor

After the Four Champions battle, Dilbert salvaged parts from [[Vanguard 7]].

He repurposed the construct into a personal combat exosuit.

Known upgrades include:

- approximately +15 AC increase
    
- major survivability
    
- additional limbs
    
- improved mobility
    
- enhanced combat capability
    

This drastically increased his battlefield threat.

Only Dilbert would look at a terrifying enemy construct and think:

> “I can improve this.”

## Relationships

### Party

Dilbert has become a trusted ally and an increasingly central member of the group.

Despite his strange nature, the party has grown to rely heavily on him.

---

### [[Zanaris]]

Dilbert has formed one of the strongest and most wholesome bonds within the party with [[Zanaris]].

Throughout much of the dungeon, Dilbert rides on Zanaris’s shoulders.

What began as practicality evolved into genuine emotional connection.

The two have developed clear:

- trust
    
- comfort
    
- affection
    
- protectiveness
    

Dilbert appears especially comfortable around Zanaris.

She, in turn, demonstrates remarkable patience, warmth, and care toward him.

Their bond provides one of the campaign’s most emotionally grounding relationships.

---

### [[Fnipper]]

Fnipper gave one of the most consequential commands involving Dilbert.

During the Four Champions battle, Fnipper ordered Dilbert to stop [[Claude the Clown]].

Dilbert complied.

Immediately.

Violently.

---

### [[Gork]]

Dilbert and Gork were both present in the dungeon city, though their long-term relationship remains unclear.

## Threat Assessment

**Threat Level: Extremely High (if hostile)**

Dilbert’s danger comes from a fatal misconception:

People judge him by appearance.

That mistake could be catastrophic.

If hostile, Dilbert would be terrifying because he combines:

- intelligence
    
- magical power
    
- engineering skill
    
- creativity
    
- preparation
    

Cute does not mean harmless.

## Memorable Moments

### Shoulder Companion

During much of the dungeon exploration, Dilbert rode on [[Zanaris]]’s shoulders.

This became one of the party’s most charming recurring images.

The contrast was incredible:

- tiny caterpillar genius
    
- calm ranger carrier
    
- lethal dungeon around them
    

Strangely wholesome.

---

### Salad Ritual

Dilbert and [[Zanaris]] frequently shared salad during dungeon exploration.

This became a recurring symbol of their friendship.

In a campaign filled with:

- cosmic horror
    
- divine punishment
    
- lethal traps
    
- murderous champions
    

A tiny caterpillar happily eating salad on a ranger’s shoulder somehow became one of the most human moments.

---

### Claude Execution

During the Four Champions battle, [[Claude the Clown]] attempted escape.

[[Fnipper]] ordered Dilbert to stop him.

Dilbert responded with:

**Power Word Kill**

Claude died instantly.

No save.  
No dramatic final stand.  
Just dead.

One of the coldest moments in the campaign.

---

### Vanguard Salvage

[[Fnipper]] repurposed [[Vanguard 7]] into personal armor for Dilbert.

An absurd and perfectly move.
