# Session 001 - Guild Hall Beginnings

Campaign: [[Campaigns/Bell Rod Vault/Notes/Campaign Overview|Bell Rod Vault]]

Date Played: 2026-01-10  
Party Level: 3

## Session Summary

The Bell Rod Vault campaign began in a guild hall, where the party first came together and the chaotic group dynamic that would define the campaign began to take shape.

At the time, the group had no idea how deeply they would become entangled in celestial conflict, soul-bound artifacts, divine curses, and cosmic mysteries.

They were just adventurers.

Or at least, that was the illusion.

This first session quietly introduced two individuals who would later become major enemies:

[[Felix the Flayer]] and [[Roland the Rejected]].

Neither appeared especially important at the time.

That would change dramatically.

What began as guild hall intrigue and petty theft would eventually evolve into one of the campaign’s most personal rivalries.

## Major Events

- Party gathered for the first time in the guild hall
    
- Initial party personalities and dynamics established
    
- [[Fnipper]] and [[Felix the Flayer]] clashed during competing theft attempts
    
- [[Roland the Rejected]] intervened after Felix was exposed
    
- Early tension with nobles during eavesdropping session by [[Fnipper]]
    

## Important Character Moments

### [[Fnipper]]

Session 001 established Fnipper exactly as expected:

Sneaky, opportunistic, and always looking for angles.

While operating under invisibility, Fnipper infiltrated areas near the nobles and engaged in theft and reconnaissance.

During these actions, Fnipper stole a tome of value.

This theft contributed to escalating chaos.

More importantly, Fnipper became entangled with another rogue operating nearby:

[[Felix the Flayer]].

Both rogues were attempting to steal the same crossbow and tome, different instances and scenarios.

This created immediate tension.

The rivalry between Fnipper and Felix effectively began here.

In hindsight, this was the first move in a feud that would culminate in one of the campaign’s most legendary kills.

---

### [[Mordikahn]]

Even early in the campaign, Mordikahn established himself as physically imposing, confident, and highly theatrical.

He used presence and intimidation as tools.

During guild hall interactions, Mordikahn also threw mead to create a distraction for Fnipper, adding to the party’s chaotic energy.

This helped establish his personality as someone who blends intimidation with dramatic flair.

---

### [[Ephram]]

Ephram contributed one of the first truly iconic party moments.

During an intimidation attempt, Ephram climbed onto Mordikahn’s shoulders to gain additional presence while confronting another individual, who later we know as [[Roland the Rejected]]

The image was absurd.

A cleric standing atop a heavily armored paladin should not feel intimidating.

Yet somehow, for this party, it worked.

This moment perfectly established the campaign’s balance between epic and ridiculous.

## Rogue Conflict

One of the most important developments of Session 001 involved the emerging conflict between Fnipper and Felix.

Both were operating under invisibility during portions of the guild hall chaos.

Both were attempting theft.

Both were skilled rogues.

That naturally created friction.

The immediate source of conflict centered around a valuable crossbow both sought to acquire.

This created a competitive standoff.

The situation worsened when Fnipper’s additional theft involving a noble-associated tome created more chaos.

This may have contributed to Felix being blamed or getting into trouble, particularly in relation to his status as an apprentice within the rogue guild.

This incident laid the foundation for their long-running hostility.
