# Session 012 - Return of Gork

Campaign: [[Campaigns/Bell Rod Vault/Notes/Campaign Overview|Bell Rod Vault]]

Date Played: 2026-06-13  
Party Level: 9

## Session Summary

Session 012 began in the aftermath of one of the party’s greatest victories.

The Four Champions had fallen.

[[Felix the Flayer]] was annihilated beyond recovery. [[Claude the Clown]] had been erased. [[Roland the Rejected]] and [[Vanguard 7]] had finally been defeated.

For the first time in a long while, the party had breathing room.

That peace did not last.

The session began with recovery and upgrades. Using salvaged components from [[Vanguard 7]], the party helped [[Dilbert]] construct a powerful armored exosuit, dramatically increasing his survivability.

The party then pushed deeper into the Bell Rod Vault and entered one of the most frustrating environmental challenges yet: [[Campaigns/Bell Rod Vault/Locations/Mind Sweepfield|Mind Sweepfield]].

The dungeon weaponized traps, movement, spatial orientation, and patience.

After barely navigating the trap gauntlet, the party encountered something far more dangerous than spikes or glyphs.

[[Visnovus]] appeared.

What followed revealed his true intentions surrounding Felix’s coin and confirmed that his interest in the party had escalated dramatically.

Moments later, the party encountered another familiar figure.

[[Gork]] had returned.

And he possessed the [[Campaigns/Bell Rod Vault/Notes/Bell Rod|Bell Rod]].

The session ended with one of the most shocking moments in the campaign so far.

Gork forcibly removed Dilbert from his armor and injected him into the sphere atop the Bell Rod as part of a mysterious anchor interaction.

Dilbert disappeared into the artifact.

## Important Character Moments

### [[Dilbert]]

This session was both Dilbert’s greatest triumph and greatest tragedy.

Early in the session, Dilbert received a major upgrade using salvaged parts from [[Vanguard 7]].

The resulting armored exosuit significantly increased his combat durability and utility.

He could now:

- deploy armor rapidly
- retract armor into storage
- operate in armored or compact mode
- better survive dungeon threats

Dilbert also proved critical in navigating Mind Sweepfield, helping disable magical hazards and assisting the party through multiple dangerous trap interactions.

Then everything changed.

During the confrontation with [[Gork]], Dilbert became the focus of a Bell Rod interaction.

Gork forcibly removed Dilbert from his armored shell.

He then placed or injected Dilbert into the sphere mounted at the top of the [[Campaigns/Bell Rod Vault/Notes/Bell Rod|Bell Rod]].

The Rod activated.

Dilbert disappeared into the artifact.

His current status is unknown.

---

### [[Mordikahn]]

This session showcased both Mordikahn’s tactical discipline and mental resilience.

During the encounter with [[Visnovus]], Mordikahn entered the chamber alongside [[Zanaris]] and [[Devon]] while [[Fnipper]] remained unable to enter.

Visnovus attempted to manipulate the situation by demanding Felix’s coin.

After Zanaris escaped with the coin, Visnovus issued a direct magical command to Mordikahn:

Retrieve the coin by any means necessary.

This could have turned the party against itself.

Instead, Mordikahn resisted.

Using **Guarded Mind**, he reversed or rejected the command and maintained control of his will.

This was a major moment.

It demonstrated that Mordikahn’s mind is becoming as dangerous as his blade.

Later, during the Visnovus encounter, Mordikahn attempted physical disruption against a powerful caster-level threat using shield bash.

The target resisted with absurd strength.

The message was clear:

The party is now dealing with enemies on a terrifying scale.

---

### [[Zanaris]]

Zanaris played a crucial role during the Visnovus encounter.

She possessed Felix’s coin, making her Visnovus’s immediate target.

When Visnovus attempted to claim it, Zanaris escaped using Misty Step with support from [[Ephram]].

Her quick action prevented Visnovus from immediately gaining the artifact.

This likely prevented catastrophic consequences.

---

### [[Fnipper]]

Fnipper experienced one of the session’s strangest mysteries.

When the party approached Visnovus’s chamber, Fnipper was unable to enter.

Why he alone was barred remains unknown.

This is highly suspicious.

Given Fnipper’s relationship with:

- [[Fortuna]]
- coin mechanics
- rogue systems
- hidden powers

the restriction may be deeply significant.

---
### [[Ephram]]

Ephram played a critical support role during the Visnovus encounter.

When Visnovus attempted to seize Felix’s coin from [[Zanaris]], Ephram helped enable Zanaris’s escape, allowing her to create distance and preserve possession of the coin.

His quick support likely prevented Visnovus from acquiring a highly dangerous artifact.

Though less flashy than some of the session’s major moments, Ephram’s intervention may have altered the outcome of the encounter entirely.

---
### [[Zava]]

Zava approached [[Campaigns/Bell Rod Vault/Locations/Mind Sweepfield|Mind Sweepfield]] exactly how everyone expected:

Aggressively.

The dungeon presented:

- puzzles
- trap sequencing
- movement manipulation
- magical disorientation

Zava’s default strategy remained simple:

**“Can I jump it?”**

Unfortunately, Mind Sweepfield responded with one of the most brutal anti-barbarian curses imaginable.

At one point, Zava triggered a magical effect that caused all of his movement to become reversed.

For Zava:

- forward became backward
- backward became forward
- left became right
- right became left

Suddenly, even basic movement became a challenge.

For a warrior who normally solves problems through instinct, aggression, and momentum, this was maddening.

The curse transformed simple navigation into chaos.

Despite repeated frustration, Zava kept pressing forward, attempting to adapt through sheer stubbornness and brute force.

In fairness, this accidentally helped the party learn more about how the trap mechanics functioned.

Mind Sweepfield tried to weaponize logic.

Zava responded with barbarian science.

Results were mixed.

---
### [[Devon]]

While Devon did not have a singular flashy spotlight moment this session, his tactical utility and preparation proved extremely valuable throughout the dungeon progression.

Devon made several smart strategic plays involving his Rings of Spell Storing, helping prepare key contingency options for the party.

These included storing and preparing spells such as:

- Dispel Magic
- Magic Circle
- other situational support magic

These preparations gave the party critical flexibility when dealing with the increasingly complex magical threats inside the Bell Rod Vault.

Devon also contributed heavily to analyzing trap mechanics within [[Campaigns/Bell Rod Vault/Locations/Mind Sweepfield|Mind Sweepfield]], helping identify patterns and techniques that improved party survivability.

One of Devon’s most valuable contributions remains his telepathic link with [[Mordikahn]].

This silent communication allowed rapid tactical coordination during high-pressure moments, enabling the two to share information and adapt without revealing plans to enemies and overcoming puzzles the dungeon brought forward.

While less visible than direct combat heroics, Devon’s planning, utility magic, and battlefield awareness played a major role in the party’s success this session.

### Mordikahn and the Macarena

In one of the most absurd moments of the campaign, Dilbert’s manipulation, by way of request from [[Zanaris]] accidentally stripped [[Mordikahn]] completely naked, causing all of his equipment and possessions to spill onto the floor.

As if that were not humiliating enough, magical effects compelled Mordikahn to begin performing the Macarena while fully nude.

The chaos only escalated when [[Fnipper]], assisted by [[Fortuna]] and the gambler’s influence, immediately began looting Mordikahn’s scattered belongings during the ordeal. The belongings were returned later, which included his Amulet of the Fallen, containing [[Colopatiron]]

The result was equal parts tactical disaster, psychological warfare, and complete table chaos.

Despite the absurdity, the moment perfectly captured one of the defining truths of the party:

**Even during cosmic-level danger, nobody is safe from humiliation.**
