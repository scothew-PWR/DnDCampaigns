## Overview

Felix the Flayer is one of the Bell Rod Vault’s most dangerous recurring enemies and arguably one of the most personally significant antagonists to the party.

A master rogue, assassin, and ambush specialist, Felix combines stealth, mobility, and lethal burst damage into a terrifying combat package.

Felix is not merely dangerous because of raw damage.

He is dangerous because he controls information.

He decides:

- when combat begins
    
- where he strikes
    
- who gets isolated
    
- when he disappears
    

He weaponizes uncertainty.

More than any other enemy, Felix represents the predatory side of rogue combat.

## Appearance

Felix carries the presence of a professional killer.

Known characteristics:

- agile build
    
- stealth-focused gear
    
- precise movement
    
- assassin energy
    
- unsettling confidence
    

Everything about Felix suggests calculation.

He rarely feels panicked.

Even under pressure, he gives the impression of someone constantly evaluating escape routes, openings, and weaknesses.

## Personality

Core traits:

- arrogant
    
- cunning
    
- predatory
    
- mocking
    
- highly intelligent
    

Felix appears to enjoy psychological warfare.

He likes controlling the emotional state of his enemies.

He provokes.  
He taunts.  
He manipulates.

Unlike berserkers or brutes, Felix doesn’t seek dominance through force alone.

He seeks superiority through control.

He wants opponents to feel:

- confused
    
- frustrated
    
- powerless
    

## Known History

Felix’s rivalry with the party began before the deeper dungeon encounters.

### Alleyway Encounter

One of the earliest major conflicts involving Felix happened outside the dungeon.

Both [[Fnipper]] and Felix, being rogues with sticky fingers and oversized egos, quickly entered a competitive pissing match.

The conflict escalated.

A fight broke out.

At one point, Felix appeared to commit suicide.

This event later proved highly suspicious.

It suggested Felix may have had access to coin-based resurrection or other survival mechanics much earlier than the party realized.

This revelation significantly increased his mystery.

## Rivalry with [[Fnipper]]

This is Felix’s defining relationship.

Felix and Fnipper feel like distorted reflections of one another.

Both are:

- rogues
    
- thieves
    
- cunning opportunists
    
- highly dangerous when underestimated
    

The difference lies in intent.

Fnipper uses chaos with loyalty to the party.

Felix weaponizes chaos purely for advantage.

Their rivalry became deeply personal.

What began as theft and ego escalated into outright hatred.

## Combat Role

Felix appears optimized for:

- stealth
    
- ambush
    
- assassination
    
- mobility
    
- burst damage
    
- target isolation
    

He is exceptionally difficult to lock down.

### Key Strengths

Felix demonstrates mastery of:

- invisibility
    
- repositioning
    
- flanking
    
- escape
    
- burst attacks
    

He is most dangerous when unseen.

## Threat Assessment

**Threat Level: Extreme**

Felix is one of the scariest enemies the party has faced because he punishes mistakes brutally.

He thrives on:

- poor positioning
    
- broken formations
    
- distracted targets
    

Against unprepared opponents, Felix can erase people.

That makes Fnipper uniquely dangerous to him.

## Memorable Moments

### First Escape

Felix vanished early in the Four Champions battle.

This immediately signaled what kind of encounter it would be.

Everyone knew:

> He’s still here somewhere.

That tension alone changed the fight.

---

### Divine Sense Counterplay

Felix’s biggest advantage—stealth—was countered by [[Mordikahn]].

Using **Divine Sense**, Mordikahn located Felix despite invisibility.

This dramatically shifted the battle.

For perhaps the first time, Felix lost information control.

That was catastrophic for him.

---

### Double Shield Charge

After locating Felix, [[Mordikahn]] shield charged him and knocked him prone.

Felix recovered.

Mordikahn did it again.

That second prone setup created the opening the party needed.

It was brutal.

---

### The Deletion

This became one of the most legendary moments in the campaign.

Through coin-linked coordination between:

- [[Mordikahn]]
    
- [[Fnipper]]
    
- [[Devon]]
    

Felix was trapped and punished.

Fnipper rolled a **natural 20**.

Because of the party’s coin mechanics and smite interactions, the resulting damage exploded.

Total damage dealt in one round:

# 262 damage

This exceeded Felix’s HP by roughly double.

Felix was not merely defeated.

He was deleted.

Utterly.

One of the most satisfying boss kills in the campaign.
